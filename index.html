What is RENEWABLE ENERGY?

Renewable energy is energy that comes from natural sources that are constantly replenished. These sources include sunlight, wind, water, and geothermal heat. Renewable energy can be used to generate electricity, heat and cool buildings, and power transportation.

RENEWABLE ENERGY Sources in INDIA

WIND RENEWABLE ENERGY

Wind renewable energy is a clean, sustainable, and renewable source of energy. It does not produce any greenhouse gases or other pollutants, and it does not require any fossil fuels. Wind energy is also a reliable and predictable source of energy, as the wind blows consistently throughout the year.

SOLAR RENEWABLE ENERGY

solar renewable energy is a clean, sustainable, and increasingly affordable source of energy that can play a significant role in addressing climate change and meeting growing energy demands. With advancements in technology and policy support, solar energy is poised to become a major contributor to the global energy landscape.

HYDROELECTRIC RENEWABLE ENERGY

Hydroelectricity, a form of renewable energy, harnesses the kinetic energy of moving water to generate electricity. This process involves directing water through turbines, causing them to spin and activate generators that produce electricity. The amount of electricity generated is determined by the volume of water and the height from which it falls.

BIOMASS RENEWABLE ENERGY

Biomass energy is also known as bioenergy. The energy produced from biomass is much healthier for the planet than non-renewable energy sources such as coal. Biomass energy can be burned directly for heat or converted to liquid and gaseous fuels through various processes. For example, wood burned in wood stoves and wood pellets used in domestic biomass boilers.
